Things You Must Know About Baking (Rules) - Baking Knowledge

Posted on April 17, 2019
viewed

Precise weighing:

Raw material calculations in baking recipes are mostly in grams (g), so be sure to use a precision electronic kitchen scale. When weighing, pay attention to the unit of measurement in the formula, whether it is gram or milliliter (ml). Many kitchen scales are now available for unit switching, or material switching (water, milk, etc.).

When weighing the material, place the container on the kitchen scale and then start the power supply to accurately weigh the material. When you need to add another material, you only need to clear the weight (usually the power button) to carry out the next weighing.The Accurate unit calculation is the first factor to ensure the success of baking!

Do not substitute raw materials:

Many green hands who have contacted baking have suffered a lot of failures. One of the important reasons is to replace all kinds of baking ingredients at will: milk is gone, can i change to yogurt? No whipping cream, can i change to milk? And so on. It is important to know that each material has its irreplaceable properties, from density to taste, and the subtle chemical and physical changes that occur during the baking process are lost.

Therefore, before testing a recipe, be sure to ensure that every raw material is in place and strictly follow the material and quantity required by the formula to ensure the correct taste of the finished product!

Strictly follow the production process:

In baking recipes, we often see cumbersome steps: “adding sugar in three times”, “adding eggs one by one”, etc., so that the precise execution process can never be omitted.

Adding raw materials at one time will cause irreparable failure. Therefore, please be sure to read each baking recipe carefully, and strictly follow the recipe requirements, and you will surely get the joy of each success.
